Existing Account Baseline

What the data
already tells us.

The account has been running. Purchases are being tracked. The pixel is firing. We are not starting from zero — we are starting from a platform that has already proven it can convert. The job now is to make it convert profitably and at scale.

Finding 1

Purchases Are Converting at €23 to €45 CPP

Two purchase-optimised campaigns generated 11 tracked pixel purchases across €369 of combined spend. Cost per purchase ranged from €23.73 on the best-performing campaign to €45.43 on the Italian-targeted campaign. At a €1 trial price point, these CPPs are too high to be profitable — but they confirm the funnel works end to end. The pixel fires, the payment goes through, and Meta can find buyers. The task is to bring CPP down, not prove it can happen.
Finding 2

Video Creative Drove the Highest Volume

The WebSite_CBO campaign generated 61,673 video ThruPlay completions at €0.004 each on €236 of spend. This is strong signal that video content is engaging the right audience and completing. However, ThruPlay volume alone does not convert to purchases — the retargeting bridge between video viewers and the purchase funnel needs to be built deliberately and tested.
Finding 3

Traffic Was Cheap But Disconnected from Purchase

Traffic campaigns generated over 5,500 link clicks and 1,400 landing page views at €0.02 to €0.07 per click across €265 of combined spend. Cheap traffic with no direct line to purchases means the funnel is leaking between click and conversion. Either the landing page is not converting, the audience is not qualified, or the remarketing layer that should have caught these visitors was never built. This gap is the primary structural fix in the new strategy.
Finding 4

Three Campaigns Spent With No Results Recorded

Purchase_COB spent €58.92 and IT Sale R5/R6/R7 spent €140.37 with zero results recorded. That is €199 of spend with no trackable outcome. The most likely cause is a tracking gap — pixel events not firing correctly on those specific campaigns or attribution windows set up inconsistently. Fixing the tracking foundation before relaunch is non-negotiable.
Total existing spend: €1,112 · Total reach: 202,048 · Tracked purchases: 11 · Untracked spend: €199. The account has traction. The strategy from here is consolidation, tracking integrity, and a funnel structure that connects traffic volume to purchase conversion efficiently.
Strategic Foundation

The principles
we build on.

Same framework as every campaign we run. Phased launch, concentrated budget, pixel warm-up before purchase optimisation, hard gates before remarketing deploys.

01 · Structure

Phased Launch Approach

Prospecting runs first. Remarketing only activates once the warm audience is large enough to actually serve ads against. No skipping steps to chase volume that isn't there yet.

02 · Algorithm

Advantage+ Audience Targeting

New pixel, no data. Letting Meta's algorithm find its own audience outperforms manual interest stacking every time on a clean account. We seed it with interest signals and let it work.

03 · Budget

Concentrated Ad Sets

Fewer, better-funded campaigns so each has a realistic chance of generating the event volume needed to exit learning. Spreading thin guarantees learning phase lock indefinitely.

04 · Pixel

Optimisation Ladder

Lead to Registration to Purchase. A new pixel needs signal volume before it can find buyers. Rushing straight to Purchase optimisation on a cold account burns budget without direction.

05 · Scaling

Incremental Budget Increases

20 to 30% increases only, and only when CPA data justifies it. No large jumps that reset the learning phase and force the algorithm to start over from scratch.

06 · Discipline

No Changes During Learning Phase

The first 7 to 14 days after any campaign launch or structural change are protected. Impatient edits during learning are the most common way to waste the first month of spend.

Creative Formats
Format 1

Short Reels (15 to 30 sec)

Dashboard screen recording showing predictions populating in real time. Accuracy ticker counting up to 71%. Live AI analysis visualisation. No voiceover claiming financial results. The product demonstrates itself. Captions are mandatory as most Meta video is watched without sound.
Format 2

Static: Data Visualisation

Clean, minimal design with the 71% accuracy stat as the hero number. Sport icons. Confidence levels. The aesthetic of a Bloomberg terminal, not a bookmaker. Analytical sports fans respond to visual language that treats them as intelligent data consumers. No loud colours, no flashing odds, no gambling iconography.
Format 3

Carousel: Sport by Sport

One sport per slide. Accuracy per sport. Number of daily predictions. Data methodology shown per discipline. This format naturally targets multi-sport fans and demonstrates the platform's breadth without needing a single line of promotional copy. Each slide is a data point, not a sales message.
What Never Appears in Creative
Odds in any format (1.85, 2.10, +150)
Win or loss framing in financial terms
Language implying money can be made
The words "bet," "betting," or "wager" in any language
Guaranteed outcome language of any kind
Specific match results framed as financial proof
Fabricated accuracy claims that exceed the verified 71% figure
Pixel Warm-Up

Optimisation ladder.
No shortcuts.

New pixel, zero history. Optimising for Purchase from day one gives the algorithm nothing to work with. The ladder builds signal volume before graduating to the harder event.

Weeks 1 to 3
Lead
Starting event
Email capture or trial page visit. Lower barrier, builds signal volume fast. Seeds the pixel with real purchase-intent behaviour. Target: 30+ Lead events per week before moving to next stage.
Weeks 4 to 6
Complete Registration
Requires: 30+ leads/week
Trial account creation. Closer to the actual conversion without requiring a completed payment. Moves the signal toward genuine purchase intent as pixel data accumulates.
Week 7 onwards
Purchase
Requires: pixel has history
The €1 trial conversion. By this point the pixel has meaningful registration and lead data to find lookalike buyers. ROAS and CPA become reliable metrics from here.
This is not a delay. This is how Meta's algorithm is designed to be ramped on a new account. Rushing to Purchase optimisation on a cold pixel is how accounts burn their first month of budget with nothing to show for it.
